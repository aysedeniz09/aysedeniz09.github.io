Public Health and Misinformation

Misinformation and public health outcomes.

This project analyzes the impact of misinformation on public health, with a focus on vaccine acceptance and public health behavior.

Twitter data from misinformation sentinels in Peru, trust, net sentiment, and vaccine acceptance from December 2020 to August 2021 (Lokmanoglu et al., 2023).
  • Lokmanoglu, A. D., Nisbet, E. C., Osborne, M. T., Tien, J., Malloy, S., Cueva Chacón, L., Villa Turek, E., & Abhari, R. (2023). Social Media Sentiment about COVID-19 Vaccination Predicts Vaccine Acceptance among Peruvian Social Media Users the Next Day. Vaccines, 11(4), 817.
  • Ophir, Y., Pruden, M. L., Walter, D., Lokmanoglu, A. D., Tebaldi, C., & Wang, R. (2022). Weaponizing reproductive rights: A mixed-method analysis of White nationalists’ discussion of abortions online. Information, Communication & Society, 1–26.
  • Walter, D., Ophir, Y., Lokmanoglu, A. D., & Pruden, M. L. (2022). Vaccine discourse in white nationalist online communication: A mixed-methods computational approach. Social Science & Medicine, 298, 114859.
